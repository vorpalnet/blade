BLADE Security

Authentication & Identity

This app configures how callers authenticate to BLADE. Settings are edited through the Configurator — this page is just an entry point and reference.

Inbound JWT single sign-on

Validates bearer tokens from your enterprise identity provider against its JWKS and maps the token's group/role claim onto the BLADE admin roles (Admin, Operator, Deployer, Monitor). It is additive to the existing FORM/BASIC login and ships disabled — turn it on per deployment once the issuer, JWKS URI, audience, and role mappings are set under jwt in this app's config.

Check it's wired up

GET /blade/security/api/v1/status reports the authenticated caller, the auth scheme used, and whether JWT is enabled.

The bigger picture

BLADE has three authentication surfaces — inbound HTTP (this realm-backed admin login), inbound SIP (a configurable trusted-core / digest model), and outbound REST (the framework's connector auth). All three, plus credential storage and TLS/mTLS, are documented in SECURITY.md.
